about

I am a final-year undergraduate at BINUS University, Indonesia, majoring in Information Systems and Management. My work focuses on applying machine learning and natural language processing to real-world datasets and problem settings, particularly in public sector and large-scale data contexts. I am interested in building data-driven systems that are both practical and socially relevant.

My research has primarily explored Natural Language Processing, including sentiment analysis, topic modeling, and predictive systems, with applications such as public service optimization and user behavior analysis. More recently, I have begun exploring Human Activity Recognition (HAR), exploring how language model–based approaches can be extended to time-series and sensor data.

I am particularly interested in understanding human behavior across different data modalities, bridging techniques from NLP and sequence modeling to develop more generalizable and context-aware systems.

Outside of research, I enjoy films, books, food, Formula 1, music, travel, and photography. I like capturing small, everyday moments through photos, and when I'm not glued to my screen, you'll find me wrapped in a blanket, watching a film, reading a book, or happily eating mangoes.
